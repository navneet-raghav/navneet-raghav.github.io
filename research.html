Research

My research interest is around ML privacy, membership inference attacks, machine unlearning, representation analysis, and trustworthy ML. I am currently working on internal audits for approximate machine unlearning in transformer text classifiers.

TRACE-UMIA

Internal representation based auditing of approximate machine unlearning

I am working on a research idea called TRACE-UMIA. It is not an unlearning algorithm. It is an audit method. The main question is simple: after approximate unlearning, do small forget sets still leave some internal trace inside the model?

Most common audits look at model outputs such as confidence, loss, entropy, or membership-inference style scores. My idea is to also check token-level hidden representations before and after approximate unlearning. For each sample, I measure how its internal token representations changed, then I check whether forget samples show a different drift pattern from retain and same-source unseen samples.

The final method I use is TRACE-MultiStat. It uses raw token-level representation drift, summarizes it with multiple simple statistics, and uses logistic regression as the audit classifier. I keep output-only and compact TC-style features only as baselines, not as part of the main TRACE method.

The safest claim from my current experiments is that TRACE-MultiStat can detect forget-specific internal representation traces that output-only audits may miss or under-detect, especially for small targeted forget sets. I do not claim that it proves deletion, gives a formal unlearning guarantee, or works as a black-box public API attack.

Current evidence

I tested TRACE-MultiStat mainly on DistilBERT and DistilRoBERTa using AG News, QNLI, and SST-2. The strongest setting so far is small forget-set auditing, especially forget-100 style settings. I also use exact retraining controls because I want to check that TRACE is not only detecting any generic model difference.

Research direction I want to continue

  • Stronger internal audits for approximate machine unlearning.
  • Better controls for small forget-set membership leakage.
  • Comparing output-only audits with internal representation based audits.
  • Extending the idea carefully to larger models or LLM settings.
