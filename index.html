Home

I am Navneet Raghav, a B.Tech Computer Science and Engineering student with a focus on Cybersecurity at National Forensic Sciences University, Lucknow. My current interests are in ML privacy, AI safety, membership inference attacks, machine unlearning, and representation analysis.

I am currently working as a Research Intern at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT BHU), Varanasi, under Prof. Harsh Kasyap. My work is mainly around auditing approximate machine unlearning using membership-inference style ideas and internal representation drift.

I am also working as a Summer Intern at NIT Warangal on smart energy systems, AI/ML based sensing, and energy-efficient monitoring.

Research

My main research direction right now is TRACE-UMIA. It is not an unlearning method. It is an audit method to check whether approximate unlearning still leaves internal traces of small forget sets inside transformer text classifiers.

I am interested in building simple and careful audits for ML privacy, especially in cases where output-only methods may miss internal signals.

Open to collaboration

I am looking for research internship or remote collaboration opportunities in ML privacy, trustworthy ML, AI safety, machine unlearning, and membership inference attacks.
